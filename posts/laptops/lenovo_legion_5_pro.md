## Introduction

The Lenovo Legion 5 Pro has earned a devoted following among performance-focused laptop buyers for a simple reason: it consistently delivers near-top-tier gaming performance at prices that undercut flashier competitors by $300–$600. The Gen 9 variant continues this formula with AMD's latest Ryzen 9 7945HX processor and NVIDIA's RTX 4070 Laptop GPU, housed in a refined 16-inch chassis that Lenovo has steadily improved since the Legion 5 Pro debuted. This is not a laptop that tries to impress with RGB lighting or a carbon-fiber lid — it is a machine that allocates its budget to the components that matter for gaming: CPU, GPU, display, and cooling headroom.

At $1,699, the Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 undercuts RTX 4070-class alternatives from ASUS (ROG Strix G15), Razer (Blade 15), and MSI by meaningful margins while matching or exceeding them in the specifications that determine in-game frame rates. For buyers who can accept a slightly thicker chassis and a less premium touchpad, it represents arguably the best value proposition in mid-to-high-range gaming laptops.

## Design & Build Quality

The Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 adopts Lenovo's clean "battle station" aesthetic — a matte dark grey chassis with sharp angles and a distinctive recessed lid hinge that creates a gap between the display assembly and the base when opened. The Y-shaped hinge design has become a Legion signature, and it serves a functional purpose: it lifts the rear of the base off the desk when the lid opens, improving airflow to the exhaust vents located at the back of the chassis.

The build uses an aluminum lid and plastic base construction. The aluminum lid is rigid and shows minimal flex under hand pressure; the plastic base is thicker and denser than budget-grade materials but does not feel as premium as the all-metal construction used by Razer or the ROG Strix G16. At 2.4 kg (5.3 lbs), weight is comparable to other 16-inch gaming laptops, though the relatively slim 23mm profile at the rear makes the machine feel more svelte than the number suggests.

Lenovo's cooling system uses two fans with a five heat-pipe array connecting CPU and GPU heatsinks. In performance mode — accessible via the Fn+Q shortcut or Legion Space software — fan speeds ramp to maintain both processors below thermal limits under sustained combined load. During 30-minute gaming sessions, CPU package temperatures stabilize around 90°C with GPU temperatures in the 78–83°C range, indicating the cooling system is working adequately without throttling. Fan noise peaks around 45–48 dB in performance mode, which is audible but lower than many RTX 4080-class alternatives.

## Display

The 16-inch QHD+ (2560×1600) display is one of the Legion 5 Pro Gen 9's clearest strengths. The 16:10 aspect ratio is now the preferred format in productivity-oriented gaming laptops, offering roughly 11% more vertical screen area than comparable 16:9 panels — useful for both game environments and application windows. At 165Hz with a 3ms gray-to-gray response time, the display eliminates ghosting artifacts in fast-motion gaming and provides smooth cursor tracking for competitive play.

Peak brightness at 500 nits is among the highest available in this price range, and 100% sRGB coverage with Delta E values typically below 2 means accurate, natural color rendition for creative work alongside gaming. Lenovo specifies a 72% NTSC gamut, which corresponds closely to sRGB in practical terms but falls short of the 100% DCI-P3 coverage on the more expensive ROG Strix G16. For users working with P3-content (film color grading, professional photography), this matters; for general gaming and everyday use, the distinction is minimal.

The display does not carry Dolby Vision certification and lacks a variable refresh rate (VRR/G-Sync) feature in its base configuration. NVIDIA G-Sync compatibility is present via driver, providing adaptive sync through the HDMI and USB-C outputs, but the internal panel does not dynamically sync below a minimum refresh floor. In practice, with frame rates typically above 100 fps at 1440p, tearing is infrequently encountered.

## Performance

AMD's Ryzen 9 7945HX is a 16-core, 32-thread processor built on TSMC's 4nm Zen 4 architecture, and it performs admirably in both single-threaded and multi-threaded workloads. Cinebench R23 multi-core scores in the 25,500–26,500 range position it competitively against Intel's Core i9-14900HX, while actually drawing less power in efficiency modes. Blender renders and Handbrake 4K H.265 encoding benefit from the 16-core thread count, completing benchmark tasks in approximately the same time as Intel equivalents.

The RTX 4070 Laptop GPU at 140W TGP is the critical performance component for gaming. At QHD+ (2560×1600) resolution, real-world gaming benchmarks show: Cyberpunk 2077 at High settings (no RT) averages around 80–90 fps; Horizon Forbidden West at Ultra averages approximately 75–85 fps; and esports titles like CS2 and Valorant run well above 165Hz at competitive settings, fully utilizing the display's refresh rate. DLSS 3 Frame Generation, available across most major titles that have implemented it, effectively doubles rendered frame rates — enabling playable ray-tracing performance in games like Alan Wake 2 and Cyberpunk 2077 at QHD+.

For creative workloads, the Ryzen 9 7945HX handles video editing, photo culling, and compilation tasks efficiently. DaVinci Resolve benefits from both the CUDA-accelerated GPU and the Ryzen's fast HEVC decode engine in silicon. The 16GB of DDR5-5200 in dual-channel provides adequate bandwidth for GPU-fed workloads, though users with memory-intensive workflows (large Photoshop documents, 3D modeling with complex scenes) should plan to upgrade to 32GB.

## Battery Life

Battery life on the Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 follows the pattern expected of an RTX 4070-class gaming laptop: adequate for moderate untethered use, but insufficient for full workday productivity without the power adapter. The 80Wh battery in balanced or quiet mode, with the dGPU switching to iGPU offload for non-3D tasks, achieves approximately 4 to 5 hours of mixed productivity use — web browsing, document editing, video calls. Enabling performance mode or launching any 3D application cuts runtime to around 60–90 minutes.

The included 300W power adapter is large and heavy, consistent with the platform's power demands. Lenovo does support USB-C charging at up to 100W, which can maintain battery level in quiet/efficiency modes and will charge the laptop slowly during light work — a practical feature for business travel where wall outlets are scarce.

## Keyboard, Trackpad & Ports

The Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 features a full-size keyboard with numpad, white RGB per-key backlighting, and 1.5mm key travel. The typing feel is one of Lenovo's better gaming keyboard executions — the key travel is deeper than the MacBook Air and most ultrabooks, and the actuation force is calibrated for comfortable extended typing sessions. Individual key illumination can be controlled through Legion Space software with multiple effect modes. The keyboard is spill-resistant per Lenovo's MIL-SPEC testing.

The touchpad is where the Legion shows its budget-conscious compromises most clearly. At approximately 12cm × 7cm with a plastic surface and physical click mechanism, it is functional but feels a generation behind the glass-surface precision trackpads on premium ultrabooks. Multi-touch gesture recognition works correctly, but the drag coefficient and surface texture are noticeably inferior to Apple's Force Touch or the glass pads on the Razer Blade 15. Gaming users who will primarily use an external mouse will barely notice; productivity users who work trackpad-first will.

The port selection is comprehensive: four USB-A 3.2 Gen 1 ports, one USB-C 3.2 Gen 2 (with DisplayPort 1.4 and 140W PD charging), one USB-C 3.2 Gen 2 (DisplayPort 1.4), one HDMI 2.1 (4K/120Hz), one 2.5Gbps Ethernet, and a 3.5mm combo audio jack. The three-sided port layout with USB-A on both sides of the chassis is thoughtful — cables naturally route away from the dominant hand during mouse use.

## Who Should Buy It?

The Lenovo Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 is the ideal choice for performance-driven gamers who are budget-conscious and unwilling to sacrifice frame rates to get there. If your primary use case is 1440p gaming and you want the best possible frames-per-dollar, this machine consistently wins the value calculation against RTX 4070 competitors. It also serves students and professionals who game heavily on evenings and weekends and need a machine that handles both college-level coursework and serious gaming without being either underpowered or bankruptingly expensive.

The Legion 5 Pro is a poor fit for users who need all-day battery life, who prioritize a premium touchpad experience, or who frequently carry their laptop without the power adapter and a gaming-intensity workload.

## Verdict

The Lenovo Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 earns its reputation as the gold standard for value-oriented gaming laptop performance. The Ryzen 9 7945HX and RTX 4070 combination at 140W TGP delivers genuine 1440p ultra-settings gaming capability, the QHD+ 165Hz display is legitimately excellent, and the cooling system sustains performance under extended load without audibly screaming. The touchpad and battery life are the price paid for this value equation, and they are real limitations. But for the gamer who wants maximum in-game performance per dollar, the Legion 5 Pro Gen 9 remains one of the most defensible purchases in the category.

**Overall Score: 8.6 / 10**
