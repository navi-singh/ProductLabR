## Introduction

The HP Pavilion 15 occupies the sweet spot of the consumer laptop market — above the cheapest entry-level machines that compromise on build quality and display, but accessible to buyers who don't need the performance of a premium ultrabook or gaming laptop. The 2024 model uses Intel's 13th-generation Core i5-1335U processor, a 15.6-inch FHD IPS display, and a chassis that borrows design cues from HP's higher-end Spectre and Envy lines at a fraction of the cost. At $549, it is a well-rounded machine for students and everyday users.

The Pavilion 15's appeal is in its completeness for the price: it includes a backlit keyboard, USB-C charging, a numeric keypad, and a fingerprint reader — features that require premium pricing on many competing brands. For users whose primary tasks are web browsing, document editing, Zoom calls, and streaming, the Pavilion 15 handles all of them without complaint.

## Performance

The Core i5-1335U is a power-efficient processor designed for thin and light laptops, with 2 performance cores (up to 4.6GHz) and 8 efficiency cores. For everyday computing tasks — multiple browser tabs, Microsoft 365 apps, video calls, light photo editing — it performs smoothly and responsively. Cinebench R23 single-core scores around 1,750 indicate competitive performance for the 15W TDP class, and multi-core scores near 8,500 are adequate for office and student workloads.

Where performance ceilings become apparent is sustained multi-threaded workloads and memory-intensive multitasking. With 8GB of DDR4-3200 RAM, keeping a dozen browser tabs open alongside Zoom and a few Office documents can push memory usage close to the limit, causing some slowdown. Upgrading to 16GB RAM (one SODIMM slot is accessible for upgrade) is recommended for users who multitask heavily.

There is no discrete GPU. Intel Iris Xe integrated graphics handles video playback, light photo editing, and casual 2D gaming without issues, but 3D gaming and GPU-accelerated creative applications (Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve) are beyond its practical capability. This is a productivity machine, not a creative workstation.

## Display

The 15.6-inch FHD IPS panel at 1920x1080 provides the viewing angle and color accuracy advantages of IPS technology at a mainstream price point. Text is sharp at 141 ppi, colors are acceptably accurate for everyday content, and viewing angles are good enough for group viewing or using the laptop at non-direct angles. The 45% NTSC color coverage (approximately 65% sRGB equivalent) is below what photo and video professionals need, but entirely adequate for document work, web browsing, and media consumption.

The 250-nit peak brightness is the display's most significant limitation. Indoors in a normally lit room, 250 nits is usable. Near windows or in bright environments, glare on the non-anti-glare coated panel becomes noticeable. Outdoor use in direct sunlight is impractical. For a laptop used primarily at a desk or in classrooms, this is a manageable limitation; for users who frequently work in varied lighting, it is worth noting.

The non-touch 60Hz refresh rate is standard for this price range and category.

## Battery Life

The 41Wh battery is on the smaller side for a 15.6-inch laptop, but the Core i5-1335U's power efficiency helps extend real-world runtimes. In mixed productivity use — web browsing, document editing, video calls — the HP Pavilion 15 delivers approximately 7–9 hours of runtime. Video streaming with screen at 50% brightness yields similar numbers. These figures make it practical for a full school day or a typical workday without access to charging.

Charging is via USB-C with the included 45W adapter, which is compact enough to fit in any bag and compatible with third-party chargers. From zero, a full charge takes approximately 2.5 hours. Fast charge is not supported, but 45W is sufficient for the 41Wh battery's capacity.

## Build Quality & Design

The HP Pavilion 15 uses a combination of aluminum on the lid and polished plastic on the body, a construction approach common in the mid-range segment. The result looks more premium than expected at the price — the aluminum lid has a quality feel in hand, and the design is clean and professional enough for office and school environments.

The plastic bottom panel and keyboard deck add some flex under pressure, particularly in the keyboard center. Build quality is solid for everyday use but does not approach the rigidity of aluminum-unibody designs. Hinges feel well-damped and open easily with one hand.

Port selection is reasonable: one USB-C 3.2 Gen 1, two USB-A 3.2 Gen 1, one HDMI 1.4, a full-size SD card reader, and a 3.5mm combo audio jack. The USB-C port supports charging and data but not DisplayPort or Thunderbolt. An Ethernet port is absent, which is common at this size and price. The SD card reader is a useful inclusion for students doing photography work.

## Who Should Buy It?

The HP Pavilion 15 is an excellent choice for:

- High school and college students who need a reliable machine for coursework, research, and video calls
- Office workers and remote employees whose primary tasks are email, documents, and web-based applications
- Budget-conscious buyers who want a laptop that looks and feels more premium than basic entry-level machines
- Users upgrading from an older, slower laptop to something current-generation at a reasonable price

It is not suited for gaming, video editing, creative professional work, or users who need to run demanding applications alongside heavy multitasking.

## Verdict

The HP Pavilion 15 is a competent, well-designed everyday laptop that delivers on its value proposition without notable weaknesses given the price. The Core i5-1335U handles everyday tasks smoothly, the USB-C charging is convenient, and the design is polished enough for professional environments. The display brightness and 8GB RAM limit are real constraints that motivated buyers should address (upgrade the RAM if you multitask). Within its intended use case, the Pavilion 15 is one of the better options at its price point.

**Overall Score: 7.8 / 10**
