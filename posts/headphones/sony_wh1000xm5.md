## Introduction

Sony has held the ANC throne in the over-ear headphone category for the better part of five years. The WH-1000XM4, its predecessor, was so dominant that many questioned whether the XM5 could improve meaningfully. The answer, after extensive testing, is: yes — though the improvements are measured rather than revolutionary.

The WH-1000XM5 arrives with a completely redesigned headband and driver architecture, a new processor (QN1 paired with an integrated V1 chip), and an eight-microphone array that represents a significant leap in both ANC and call quality performance. At $349, it occupies the top tier of the premium wireless headphone market alongside the Bose QuietComfort 45 and Apple AirPods Max.

We tested the XM5 over eight weeks across commuter rail, open-plan offices, airplanes, and home listening sessions to get a complete picture of its performance in the scenarios that matter most.

---

## Design & Comfort

The XM5 marks Sony's most dramatic physical redesign of the series. Gone is the articulated foldable headband of its predecessors; the XM5 uses a smooth, minimalist single-piece construction with two sliders. The headband itself is covered in synthetic leather stretched over a soft foam core, and the ear cushions use a newly formulated foam that is noticeably more pliable than the XM4's pads.

At 250 grams, the XM5 is slightly lighter than the XM4 (254g) and meaningfully lighter than the Bose QuietComfort Ultra (254g) and Sony's own XM4. The weight distribution feels balanced — extended listening sessions of two or three hours produce minimal fatigue on the head or ears. The ear cups are larger and deeper than the XM4's, which benefits listeners with larger ears and slightly improves passive isolation before ANC is even activated.

The trade-off is portability. The XM4 could fold flat and compact into a smaller case footprint. The XM5 folds flat but not into the same compact bundle, and the included soft carry case offers meaningfully less protection than the XM4's semi-rigid molded shell. For frequent travelers, this is a genuine regression. For daily commuters who keep headphones in a bag or backpack, it is an acceptable compromise.

Touch controls cover the right ear cup — swipe for track and volume, tap for play/pause, hold for voice assistant or ANC toggling. The controls are intuitive but sensitive; thick hair, glasses, or even a winter hat brushing the ear cup can trigger accidental skips.

## Sound Quality

The XM5's most transformative upgrade over the XM4 is its audio signature. The new 30mm drivers — co-developed specifically for this model — deliver a tighter, more accurate sound than any previous XM-series headphone. The default sound profile through the Sony Headphones Connect app is balanced, with only the gentlest mid-bass warmth and a slightly elevated presence region that keeps vocals crisp and forward without brightness fatigue.

At the top of the performance hierarchy is LDAC. Sony's proprietary codec streams at up to 990 kbps in high-quality mode — approximately three times the bandwidth of AAC and close to six times SBC. Listening to 24-bit/96kHz master recordings on Tidal or Amazon Music HD via an Android device with LDAC support, the XM5 resolves detail that simply does not survive the AAC codec pipeline. Piano note decay, the texture of acoustic guitar strings, the spatial positioning of instruments in a well-recorded jazz recording — all are meaningfully improved.

Without LDAC (on iOS, which caps at AAC), the XM5 still sounds excellent for the category. The bass response reaches cleanly down to around 20 Hz in practical listening, with the sub-bass remaining controlled and textured rather than boomy. Treble extension is smooth through 15 kHz, with the XM5 avoiding the peaky harshness that some competing headphones exhibit.

The soundstage is moderate — as is typical for closed-back over-ears, imaging is not wide. The DSEE Extreme upscaling feature, which uses AI to reconstruct compressed audio, is subtly effective on streaming services and becomes a set-and-forget improvement for the majority of listening.

## Noise Cancellation

This is where the WH-1000XM5 stands alone. The eight-microphone system — four dedicated to ANC and four to call quality — combined with the HD Noise Cancelling Processor QN1 delivers measured attenuation of approximately 28 to 30 dB across the key frequency range of 50 Hz to 1 kHz. In practical terms:

- On a commuter train, engine rumble and rail noise are reduced to a distant hum
- In a coffee shop, the ambient chatter of surrounding conversations drops to an unintelligible murmur
- On an aircraft, cabin roar attenuates to the point where conversation and music both feel more private than at any competing price point

The XM5's ANC is most effective on low-to-mid frequency noise sources — exactly the environments (transit, planes, open offices) most people use ANC headphones for. It is somewhat less dominant on high-pitched noise sources like high-frequency machinery whine or some human voices, where the Bose QuietComfort Ultra closes the gap.

Adaptive Sound Control uses the accelerometer and GPS activity to automatically switch between ANC profiles based on whether you are sitting, walking, or on transit — a genuinely convenient automation for daily commuters.

---

## Call Quality & Features

The four dedicated call microphones represent the largest functional improvement over the XM4. The AI-powered beamforming algorithm isolates the voice while attenuating background noise, and in third-party testing the XM5 consistently ranks among the top performers for call intelligibility in noisy environments.

Speak-to-Chat is one of the more genuinely useful gimmick-to-feature evolutions in consumer headphones. When the XM5 detects you speaking — not just any sound, but specifically vocal speech directed outward — it automatically pauses playback and switches to Ambient Mode so you can conduct a brief conversation without physically removing the headphones or pressing any button. The detection is quick (typically under 0.5 seconds) and the false trigger rate is low in our testing. The feature automatically reverts to ANC and resumes playback after a configurable delay (30 seconds to 3 minutes) of silence.

## Battery Life

The 30-hour battery with ANC enabled is one of the XM5's strongest competitive differentiators. In real-world testing at moderate volume with ANC active, we consistently achieved 28 to 32 hours depending on codec — LDAC draws slightly more power than AAC. With ANC off, rated life extends to 40 hours.

The quick charge capability is genuinely impressive: three minutes on a USB-C cable delivers enough charge for three hours of listening. This is a real get-out-of-jail feature for travelers and commuters who forget to charge. A full charge from empty takes approximately 3.5 hours.

---

## Who Should Buy It?

The Sony WH-1000XM5 is the right choice for:

- **Daily commuters and travelers** who need the best available ANC in a comfortable form factor they can wear for hours
- **Android users with LDAC-capable devices** who want to extract maximum audio quality from their wireless listening
- **Work-from-home or open-office users** who need isolation and clear call quality simultaneously
- **Music listeners who want quality across genres** — the balanced sound profile serves pop, jazz, classical, and electronic equally well

It is a harder sell for audiophiles who want the most open, expansive soundstage possible (open-back wired headphones serve that need better), for users who prioritize portability (the XM4 was more packable), or for iOS users who cannot use LDAC.

---

## Verdict

The Sony WH-1000XM5 earns its position as the default recommendation for anyone shopping at the $300–$350 ANC headphone tier. The ANC is the best available in a portable over-ear form factor, the sound quality with LDAC is genuinely audiophile-adjacent, and the 30-hour battery is essentially inexhaustible for normal use. The non-folding design and soft carry case are real trade-offs. They are not dealbreakers. This is the headphone most people should buy.
