## Introduction

Bose invented the consumer noise-cancelling headphone category. The QuietComfort series, which dates to the early 2000s, has been the preferred companion of business travelers and open-office workers for two decades. The QuietComfort 45, released in 2021, represents the latest evolution of this lineage — modernized with USB-C charging, improved drivers, and a refreshed app experience, while retaining the legendary fit and comfort that generations of QC buyers have trusted.

Against newer competition including the Sony WH-1000XM5 and Bose's own higher-end QuietComfort Ultra, the QC45 occupies a specific position: best-in-category comfort, excellent ANC, competitive price, and a no-fuss simplicity of use that sophisticated feature sets cannot replace for some buyers. At $279 (frequently on sale for $229 or less), it often represents the best value in the premium ANC headphone tier.

We tested the QC45 over seven weeks alongside the Sony XM5 and Apple AirPods Max for a direct comparison across the most relevant use cases.

---

## Sound Quality

The QC45's sound signature is warm and full-bodied — a characteristic Bose house sound that has proven commercially successful for good reason. Bass is present and forward in the mix without overwhelming the mid-range; vocals sit clearly in the center of the image; treble is smooth and forgiving without significant detail retrieval above 10 kHz.

This tuning serves pop, R&B, electronic, and lo-fi listening extremely well. It is less ideal for classical, jazz, or acoustic genres where transient accuracy, air, and separation matter more. The EQ sliders in the Bose Music app allow adjustment of low, mid, and high frequencies, and the difference between the stock profile and a flatter, more neutral setting is meaningful for critical listening.

The absence of LDAC or aptX is the most significant technical limitation. At $279, competing with the Sony WH-1000XM5 ($349) means accepting that the XM5 delivers substantially better codec performance with LDAC-capable Android devices. For iPhone users on Apple Music — where AAC 256 kbps is the delivery format — the gap is narrower and less practically meaningful.

---

## Noise Cancellation

The QC45's ANC system uses six microphones across the two ear cups in a feedforward/feedback configuration that delivers excellent attenuation of the low-to-mid frequency noise sources that matter most in travel and work environments. In third-party measurements, the QC45 consistently achieves approximately 22–25 dB of average attenuation across 50 Hz to 1 kHz — trailing the Sony XM5 by a few decibels at the low end but matching or exceeding it at mid-range frequencies.

In practical testing:
- On commuter trains, engine rumble and ambient passenger noise were reduced to comfortable background levels
- In open offices, HVAC noise and keyboard clatter became largely inaudible
- On aircraft, cabin roar was significantly attenuated — not quite to the degree of the XM5, but sufficient that music playback felt private and quiet

Aware Mode passes through environmental sound for situational awareness. It works adequately for understanding announcements and holding brief conversations, but the processing produces a slight telephonic quality that the Apple AirPods Max's Transparency mode avoids. For users who switch frequently between ANC and awareness modes, the XM5 or AirPods Max deliver more natural transparency.

## Comfort & Design

Comfort is where the QC45 establishes undisputed leadership. At 238 grams — the lightest headphone in this comparison group — combined with the QC series' characteristically gentle clamping force and generously padded memory foam ear cushions, the QC45 can be worn for four, five, or six hours without the headache, ear fatigue, or neck strain that heavier headphones produce.

The ear cushions use protein leather over memory foam in an oval shape that accommodates a wide range of ear sizes without contact pressure. The headband distributes weight evenly, and the hinged ear cup mechanism allows the cups to rotate and tilt to conform to any head shape. Bose's consistency in this area over decades of refinement shows — the QC45 fit is immediately comfortable for virtually every user from the first wear.

The folding flat design is a practical advantage over the Sony XM5. The QC45 folds into its included hard-shell case — a zippered rigid case that is meaningfully better than the XM5's soft pouch for bag throws and travel. For frequent flyers who care about gear protection, this distinction matters.

Physical buttons — rather than touch controls — are used for volume, playback, and ANC mode switching. This feels anachronistic against competitors with polished touch surfaces, but it also means zero accidental trigger rate. Hair, glasses, winter hats, and hands-on adjustments never cause unintended inputs.

---

## Features & Connectivity

The Bose Music app provides EQ adjustment, Aware Mode sensitivity control, and firmware updates. It is functional and stable, if less feature-rich than Sony's Headphones Connect, which offers parametric EQ, adaptive sound control, and more granular ANC customization.

Multipoint Bluetooth allows simultaneous pairing to two devices — switching between a laptop and phone is handled automatically. Voice assistants including Google Assistant, Amazon Alexa, and Siri are accessible via dedicated button hold. There is no wear detection or automatic pause when headphones are removed; this is a minor quality-of-life omission that more expensive headphones address.

---

## Battery Life

Twenty-four hours with ANC active is strong performance and closely consistent with manufacturer claims in real-world testing. Battery life does not degrade meaningfully with ANC enabled versus disabled, which simplifies usage without requiring ANC management to preserve charge.

The 15-minute quick charge providing three hours of playback is less impressive than the Sony XM5's 3-minute charge for 3 hours, but it remains functional for hurried departures. A full charge from empty takes approximately 2.5 hours via USB-C.

---

## Who Should Buy It?

- **Travelers and commuters who prioritize all-day wearing comfort** above any other attribute
- **Users who want excellent ANC without the Sony XM5 premium** — the QC45 frequently sells for $229 on sale, creating exceptional value
- **iPhone and laptop users** who rely on AAC and do not need LDAC
- **Users who want physical buttons** rather than touch controls that trigger accidentally
- **Frequent flyers** who pack headphones in checked bags or backpacks and need the hard case protection

Not the best choice for Android audiophiles seeking hi-res wireless audio, users who want the most natural transparency mode, or buyers who need automatic wear detection.

---

## Verdict

The Bose QuietComfort 45 may not lead any single specification category, but it delivers a consistently excellent experience across comfort, ANC, sound quality, and portability at a competitive price. For the listener who will wear headphones for six-hour travel days and values arriving without a tension headache, the QC45 remains the most practical recommendation in the ANC headphone category. The Sony XM5 is technically superior; the QC45 is arguably more livable.
