## Introduction

Not every robot vacuum needs to map your home, identify obstacles by AI camera, or wash its own mop pads in a $300 dock. For households that want reliable, low-hassle daily vacuuming on a budget, the Eufy RoboVac 11S has quietly become the standard recommendation for years. At $149 and 2.85 inches tall, it reaches floor space that no other category of robot vacuum can access, operates at a near-whisper, and runs for a genuine 100 minutes on a charge. It does not mop, it does not map, and it does not learn. It cleans—and for the price, it cleans well.

## Design & Setup

The RoboVac 11S is as close to a plug-and-play appliance as robot vacuums get. Remove it from the box, place the charging dock against a wall with clearance on both sides, plug in the dock, and set the robot on it to charge for a few hours before first use. There is no app required (though the EufyHome app adds scheduling), no Wi-Fi configuration at setup, and no mapping run. Control via the included IR remote allows start, stop, and mode selection—Auto, Edge, Single Room, and Spot modes—from the couch without any smartphone involvement.

The design is the classic Eufy low-profile disc. The top surface has a single physical button for manual start/stop, a narrow bumper ring, and no protrusions that would catch under low furniture. The 2.85-inch height is its most important physical attribute: the overwhelming majority of sofas, platform beds, and low-clearance furniture sit between 3.5 and 5 inches off the floor, and the RoboVac 11S fits under all of them without modification. Competitors with taller profiles—including most LiDAR-equipped flagships at 3.5 to 4 inches—are excluded from these under-furniture areas where dust and pet hair accumulate most heavily.

## Cleaning Performance

On hard floors—hardwood, tile, laminate, vinyl—the RoboVac 11S performs with full competence. The 1300 Pa suction handles everyday debris including dust, crumbs, pet hair, and small food particles reliably. The rolling brush agitates the floor surface to loosen particulate, and the dual side brushes sweep wall-adjacent debris into the suction path for edge cleaning that is genuinely effective for a budget robot.

On low-pile carpet, the 11S transitions smoothly and delivers adequate daily-maintenance cleaning. It removes surface debris and light pet hair from low carpet fibers without issue, though it does not produce the deep-extraction results of higher-powered competitors. On medium-pile carpet, the 1300 Pa suction becomes a real limitation—the robot cleans the surface layer but does not pull embedded particulate from within the pile. For primarily carpeted homes with medium or thick pile, a more powerful robot is the right tool.

The pet hair performance on hard floors is one of the 11S's better attributes. The rolling brush is designed to minimize tangling, and the large dustbin—0.6 liters—holds a meaningful amount of debris before requiring emptying.

## Navigation & Coverage

The RoboVac 11S uses random-walk navigation with gyroscope assistance and wall-following edge detection rather than a mapped navigation system. In practice this means the robot moves in patterns it adapts based on bump sensor inputs: generally straight lines that redirect on contact, dedicated wall-following for edge passes, and a tightening spiral for the Spot cleaning mode. Over a 100-minute run, the coverage of a typical room or apartment is adequate for daily maintenance—not perfect geometric coverage, but sufficient to address the majority of the floor surface.

Rooms with complex furniture layouts, many obstacles, or significant under-furniture real estate will be cleaned less systematically than a mapped robot would achieve. The robot can get stuck under low furniture with cable clutter, wedge into narrow gaps, and occasionally repeat coverage of the same path while missing an adjacent area. These are the predictable limitations of gyroscope-only navigation, and experienced robot vacuum users generally manage them by removing obstacles before runs.

There are no virtual walls or no-go zone features in the base RoboVac 11S configuration. Eufy sells infrared virtual boundary strips separately, which are useful for keeping the robot out of specific areas like pet feeding stations or fragile object groupings.

## App & Smart Features

The EufyHome app (iOS and Android) adds Wi-Fi scheduling and remote start/stop capabilities to the RoboVac 11S when the robot's Wi-Fi module is enabled. The app interface is clean and functional—set a cleaning schedule for specific days and times, start or stop a run remotely, check battery status, and receive notifications when a run completes or the robot is stuck. It is not a feature-rich smart mapping application, but it covers the practical scheduling use case well.

Alexa and Google Assistant voice control are supported through the EufyHome skill integrations, enabling commands like "Alexa, ask Eufy to start cleaning" without opening the app. For smart home households that already use voice assistants, this makes the 11S feel more integrated than its price point suggests.

## Who Should Buy It?

The Eufy RoboVac 11S is the right robot vacuum for apartment dwellers and small-home owners with primarily hard floors or low-pile carpet who want daily automated cleaning without the complexity or cost of a flagship model. It is particularly compelling for renters, first-time robot vacuum buyers, and users who find complex app-driven mapping systems more friction than they want. The ultra-slim profile also makes it the specific recommendation for any household with low-clearance furniture where taller robots literally cannot go.

It is not the right choice for homes where medium-to-thick carpet predominates, households that need mopping capability, or users who want systematic room-by-room mapping and zone-based scheduling.

## Verdict

The Eufy RoboVac 11S has survived multiple generations of robot vacuum innovation not because it has the most features, but because it does what it does—daily automated vacuuming on hard floors and low carpet—reliably, quietly, and at a price point anyone can justify. The 2.85-inch profile and near-silent operation are genuinely differentiated features that no amount of additional budget eliminates as needs. For its intended use case, it remains the cleanest, simplest, and most accessible entry point into robot vacuum ownership.
