## Introduction

Robot vacuums have existed for two decades, but the Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra represents something genuinely new: a system so capable and self-sufficient that the manual labor of floor care approaches zero. With 10,000 Pa of suction power—the highest in Roborock's lineup—ReactiveAI 2.0 obstacle avoidance using a real RGB camera, VibraRise 2.0 sonic mopping that scrubs at 3000 RPM and lifts automatically when it detects carpet, and a docking station that empties the dustbin, refills the water tank, washes the mop pads, and dries them afterward, the S8 MaxV Ultra is the most complete automated floor care system on the consumer market.

At $1,599, it demands serious investment. This review will tell you whether the capability justifies the cost, what compromises remain, and who this product is truly built for.

## Setup & App

Unboxing and initial setup is straightforward but time-consuming due to the dock's complexity. The dock unit itself is substantial—roughly the size of a small printer—and needs to be positioned near both a power outlet and a water source (or manually filled water reservoir). The clean water and dirty water tanks are user-removable for filling and emptying at a sink, which Roborock estimates needs attention every one to two weeks depending on cleaning frequency.

The Roborock app (iOS and Android) guides initial Wi-Fi pairing with a simple QR code process and supports both 2.4GHz and 5GHz networks. First-time mapping takes approximately 20-40 minutes depending on home size, during which the robot uses its PreciSense LiDAR system to generate a detailed floor plan. The resulting map can be edited in the app to add room labels, set virtual no-go zones, designate carpet-only or mop-only areas, and configure per-room cleaning preferences including suction power level and mop water flow rate.

The app is one of the most feature-complete in the robot vacuum category. Scheduled cleaning, zone cleaning, selective room cleaning, and multi-floor map management are all available. The cleaning history log shows each run's path, duration, area covered, and suction mode used—useful for verifying the robot is performing as expected. Voice assistant integration supports both Amazon Alexa and Google Assistant.

## Vacuuming Performance

The S8 MaxV Ultra's 10,000 Pa suction is not a marketing number that disappears in practice—it is genuinely and noticeably powerful. On hard floors, it captures fine dust, pet dander, and debris particles that lesser robots leave behind in multiple passes. On low-pile carpet, it extracts embedded dirt that requires multiple conventional passes from lower-powered competitors. On medium-pile carpet, the suction improvement is most dramatic: where many robots skim the surface, the S8 MaxV Ultra pulls particulate from within the pile in a single pass.

The dual rubber brush system—two counter-rotating rubber rollers rather than the traditional bristle brush—is a significant practical improvement for pet owners and anyone with long hair in the household. Bristle brushes trap hair and require frequent manual cleaning to maintain performance; the rubber rollers guide hair to the sides for collection rather than entangling it. After weeks of testing, the brushes required minimal maintenance compared to competitors.

Edge cleaning performance is strong. A dedicated side brush pushes wall-adjacent debris toward the suction path, and the robot's mapping intelligence ensures it makes dedicated edge passes along walls and furniture. Corner coverage is not perfect—no circular robot is—but the coverage is as good as any current competitor.

## Mopping Performance

The VibraRise 2.0 mopping system is the most advanced mop implementation in any consumer robot. The dual mop pads oscillate at 3000 RPM in a horizontal scrubbing motion, applying significant mechanical friction to the floor surface rather than simply dragging a damp cloth. The practical effect is that dried-on stains, light grease, and tracked-in grime that conventional robot mops merely spread around are actually broken up and removed.

The VibraRise system's automatic carpet detection and lift mechanism is critical to the system's real-world usability. The moment the S8 MaxV Ultra's sensors detect carpet, the mop assembly rises 5mm off the ground, preventing wet mop pads from saturating carpet fibers. In homes with mixed hard floor and carpet areas—which is most homes—this eliminates the need to set up no-mop zones manually or separate cleaning runs for different surfaces.

Stubborn stains may require a second pass or targeted spot cleaning, and the mopping system is not a substitute for occasional hand-mopping of heavily soiled areas. But for daily and weekly maintenance of kitchen, bathroom, and living area hard floors, the results are genuinely impressive and require no user involvement after initial scheduling.

## Navigation & Obstacle Avoidance

ReactiveAI 2.0 is a multi-sensor obstacle avoidance system combining an RGB camera, structured light sensor, and the primary LiDAR unit. In practice, this means the S8 MaxV Ultra identifies objects it encounters rather than simply avoiding them as anonymous obstacles. The system recognizes shoes, bags, cables, toys, and—critically for pet owners—animal waste, with industry-leading reliability compared to infrared-only obstacle avoidance systems.

In our testing, the robot successfully avoided loose USB cables, scattered children's toys, and dog food bowls on every observed run. False positive avoidances—cases where the robot stopped for a non-existent obstacle—were rare and mostly related to low-contrast objects in poor lighting. The system performs notably better in normally lit rooms than in very dark environments, where the camera's effectiveness is reduced.

Mapping via PreciSense LiDAR is fast and accurate. Multi-room homes are mapped in a single exploratory run, and the resulting floor plan is detailed enough to use as-is in most cases. In homes with complex layouts featuring many small rooms, narrow corridors, or large open spaces with sparse furniture, occasional manual room boundary corrections may be needed in the app. But for typical residential layouts, the autonomous mapping is reliable.

## Docking Station & Maintenance

The all-in-one dock is the feature that most meaningfully separates the S8 MaxV Ultra from lower-tier robot vacuums. After each cleaning run, the robot returns to the dock, which automatically empties the dustbin into a sealed bag (holding up to 7 weeks of debris), refills the robot's water tank from the dock's clean water reservoir, scrubs the mop pads with clean water and a built-in cleaning brush, and then hot-air dries the pads to prevent mold and mildew. The entire post-run process is autonomous.

The practical effect is that the user's direct maintenance tasks reduce to: replacing the dustbin bag every 7 weeks, refilling the dock's clean water tank, and emptying the dirty water tank—both water tasks approximately every 1-2 weeks. For a household running daily cleaning cycles, this represents a dramatic reduction in hands-on maintenance compared to robots that require manually emptying the dustbin after every run and cleaning mop pads by hand.

The dock's mop cleaning and drying cycle does generate noise—roughly equivalent to a quiet dishwasher cycle. Scheduling cleaning runs to conclude before nighttime quiet hours is advisable.

## Who Should Buy It?

The Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra is built for homeowners who want genuinely automated floor maintenance and are willing to pay for it. Pet owners, families with young children, and anyone with a mix of hard floors and carpets across a medium to large home will find the greatest value. The obstacle avoidance capability makes it far more reliable in cluttered, lived-in spaces than cameras-and-infrared-only competitors, and the fully automated dock reduces the mental overhead of robot vacuum ownership to near zero.

It is less suitable for apartment dwellers with small, simple floor plans where the dock's footprint is disproportionate to the living space, or for buyers whose budget makes the $1,599 price point a stretch—capable competitors exist at $600-$900 that cover the core use case with fewer automation features.

## Verdict

The Roborock S8 MaxV Ultra is the best robot vacuum and mop system available today for users who want maximum automation and capability. The 10,000 Pa suction is the most powerful on the consumer market, the VibraRise 2.0 mopping genuinely cleans rather than merely dampens floors, ReactiveAI 2.0 obstacle avoidance handles the real obstacles that exist in real homes, and the self-emptying, self-refilling, self-cleaning dock makes the system as close to truly hands-free as current technology allows. The price is high, and the dock requires space and occasional attention, but the total system performance earns its premium. If you're ready to nearly eliminate floor cleaning from your weekly routine, the S8 MaxV Ultra delivers.
