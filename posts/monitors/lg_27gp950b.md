## Introduction

When LG launched the 27GP950-B, it arrived at a pivotal moment: the console gaming generation had just made the leap to 4K 120Hz, and PC gamers were clamoring for a monitor that could keep up with the most demanding titles without sacrificing color quality. The UltraGear 27GP950-B answers that call emphatically. With a NanoIPS panel running at up to 160Hz, genuine HDMI 2.1 connectivity, and near-complete DCI-P3 coverage, this 27-inch display is one of the most versatile 4K monitors on the market. Whether you're playing Cyberpunk 2077 on a PS5, editing video footage, or grinding ranked matches at high frame rates, the 27GP950-B positions itself as a legitimate do-everything solution for demanding users.

At $799, it sits in the premium tier of 27-inch gaming monitors—but the feature set justifies the ask for those who need what it offers. In this review, we'll break down every aspect of the 27GP950-B to help you decide whether it belongs on your desk.

## Design & Build

The 27GP950-B carries LG's familiar UltraGear aesthetic: a matte black chassis, slim bezels on three sides, and a bold, angular stand that communicates its gaming intentions without resorting to gaudy RGB accents. The rear panel does include a small RGB lighting zone that can sync with LG's Sphere Lighting 2.0 feature, casting an ambient glow on the wall behind the monitor—it's subtle enough for those who want it, and easily disabled for those who don't.

Build quality is solid throughout. The plastic panels feel dense and well-fitted, and the stand assembly is straightforward—a simple tool-free click-in mechanism gets you up and running in minutes. The base footprint is relatively wide at roughly 24 inches across, so smaller desks may feel the squeeze, but for most standard setups it sits comfortably.

Ergonomics are a genuine strength here. The stand offers height adjustment up to 110mm, tilt from -5° to +15°, swivel ±15°, and full portrait pivot. This level of flexibility is often missing from gaming-focused monitors, which frequently ship with limited-adjustment stands. It's a welcome inclusion and makes the 27GP950-B more comfortable for long sessions whether you're sitting upright or reclining.

## Display Quality

The NanoIPS panel is the heart of this monitor, and it performs extremely well. At its native 3840×2160 resolution, pixel density sits at approximately 163 PPI—enough to render text crisply and make fine in-game details pop without individual pixels being discernible at a normal viewing distance of two feet. Colors are vibrant and accurate out of the box, with 98% DCI-P3 coverage giving images a richness and depth that standard sRGB-only panels simply cannot replicate.

Peak brightness in SDR mode is rated at 400 cd/m², which is sufficient for well-lit rooms. In HDR mode, the panel boosts to 600 cd/m² peak luminance, meeting VESA's DisplayHDR 600 certification. Highlights and bright scene elements genuinely benefit from this headroom—explosions, sunlit environments, and neon lighting in games look noticeably more dynamic than on DisplayHDR 400-certified panels.

However, it's worth being honest about the HDR limitations. The 27GP950-B uses edge-lit local dimming rather than a full-array backlight, which means black levels in HDR content remain constrained by the panel's native 1000:1 contrast ratio. Dark scenes in HDR don't reach the inky blacks of OLED or mini-LED displays. For the absolute best HDR experience, you'll need to look at more expensive options—but for most gaming content, the 27GP950-B's HDR still looks substantially better than SDR.

## Gaming Performance

This is where the 27GP950-B truly earns its UltraGear badge. The combination of 160Hz refresh rate, 1ms GtG response time, and AMD FreeSync Premium Pro (with G-Sync Compatibility certification) creates an exceptionally smooth and tear-free gaming experience. At 4K 160Hz, motion is stunningly fluid in games that can push those frame rates—competitive shooters and fast-paced action titles feel immediately different compared to a 60Hz display.

The 1ms GtG response time spec is measured in the fastest overdrive setting, which can introduce minor inverse ghosting in some transitions. In practice, using the "Fast" overdrive preset rather than "Fastest" eliminates the issue for most content while keeping response times excellent. LG's overdrive implementation is well-tuned compared to many competitors.

For console gaming, the HDMI 2.1 ports are the standout feature. Both HDMI inputs support full 4K 120Hz bandwidth—no compromises, no hidden limitations. Connecting a PS5 or Xbox Series X delivers native 4K at 120Hz with Variable Refresh Rate enabled through the console's display settings. This is genuinely rare among 27-inch gaming monitors and gives the 27GP950-B a meaningful advantage over panels that ship with HDMI 2.0 ports and cap out at 4K 60Hz on consoles.

Input lag is measured at approximately 1ms at 160Hz, which is imperceptible in any real-world gaming scenario.

## Color Accuracy & Productivity

Despite its gaming focus, the 27GP950-B is a capable productivity and content creation tool. The 98% DCI-P3 gamut is broad enough to be meaningful for photo editing, video grading, and graphic design work—especially if your output targets digital platforms and streaming services that master in DCI-P3 or HDR10.

Out of the box, the sRGB color mode is accurate with a Delta E average under 2, which is within acceptable tolerances for most casual creative work. The monitor ships with a factory calibration report, and LG's on-screen display provides an sRGB emulation mode that clamps the gamut for accurate web and SDR content viewing.

For serious colorists who need sub-1 Delta E accuracy, the 27GP950-B will likely still require hardware calibration with a colorimeter. But for users who want one monitor for both gaming and creative work without switching hardware, it strikes a genuinely useful balance.

## Connectivity

The connectivity complement on the 27GP950-B is thoughtfully assembled. The two HDMI 2.1 ports allow you to keep both a PC graphics card and a console connected simultaneously, switching between them without unplugging cables. The single DisplayPort 1.4 output handles PC gaming at full 4K 160Hz. Two USB-A 3.0 ports on the rear of the stand act as a basic USB hub—convenient for keyboards, mice, or charging accessories.

Notably absent is USB-C with display input or any power delivery functionality, which keeps the 27GP950-B firmly in the gaming camp rather than straddling the line with productivity monitors. If a single cable to a laptop is a priority for you, the Dell U2723D reviewed elsewhere is a better fit. But for a dedicated gaming or desktop workstation setup, the port selection covers all the important bases.

## Who Should Buy It?

The LG 27GP950-B is purpose-built for two types of users: PC gamers who want the best 4K gaming experience at 27 inches, and console gamers who want to take full advantage of their PS5 or Xbox Series X without compromising on refresh rate. Its blend of speed, color coverage, and genuine HDMI 2.1 support makes it one of the most capable all-around gaming monitors at its size.

It is less ideal for users who primarily need a single-cable laptop docking solution, or for strict professional colorists who need factory-calibrated hardware accuracy. And for pure competitive gaming where frame rate matters more than resolution, a 1440p 240Hz panel would serve better at a lower price.

## Verdict

The LG 27GP950-B UltraGear is one of the finest 27-inch 4K gaming monitors available. It delivers on every key metric that matters to high-end gamers: blazing-fast 160Hz refresh rate, genuine 1ms response, excellent color with 98% DCI-P3 coverage, and—crucially—real HDMI 2.1 ports that make it the ideal companion for next-gen consoles. The ergonomic stand, solid build quality, and dual-purpose gaming and creative performance round out a package that is hard to beat at this size. Minor compromises around HDR contrast depth and the absence of USB-C are manageable for most buyers. At $799, the 27GP950-B is a premium investment that delivers premium results.
