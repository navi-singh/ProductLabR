## Introduction

Dell's UltraSharp line has long been the reference standard for professional productivity monitors, and the U2723D represents the pinnacle of that legacy at 27 inches. Where gaming monitors chase frame rates and response times, the U2723D focuses on what creative professionals and power users actually need: exceptional color accuracy, a rich contrast ratio for an IPS panel, and a connectivity suite that can replace a whole shelf of docks and adapters. The headline feature is Dell's proprietary IPS Black panel technology, which achieves a 2000:1 contrast ratio—twice the typical IPS specification—bringing this display meaningfully closer to the deep blacks historically associated with VA panels while retaining the color accuracy and wide viewing angles IPS is known for.

At $599, the U2723D occupies the upper tier of its category. This review will help you understand exactly what you're paying for, who it's designed for, and where its limitations lie.

## Design & Ergonomics

The U2723D follows Dell's restrained, professional design language with quiet confidence. The chassis is matte black with thin bezels on three sides and a slightly thicker bottom bezel housing the Dell logo. There are no RGB accents, no sharp angles trying to look aggressive—just clean, functional industrial design that fits equally well in a creative studio or a corporate office.

The stand is exceptional. Dell's UltraSharp stands are consistently among the best in the industry, and the U2723D's is no exception. Users get height adjustment up to 150mm, tilt from -5° to +21°, swivel ±30°, and full portrait pivot. The adjustment mechanisms are smooth and well-damped, holding their position without drift. The monitor is also VESA 100×100 compatible, allowing arm mounting if preferred.

Build quality is excellent throughout. The plastic panels feel premium and tight, the stand base is weighted appropriately, and cable management channels in the stand neck keep the desk tidy. Dell ships the U2723D with a height-adjustable stand, USB-C cable, DisplayPort cable, and power brick in the box—a level of completeness that not all monitor makers match.

## Display Quality

The IPS Black panel is the technological centerpiece of the U2723D and the primary reason to choose it over competing 27-inch 4K displays. Standard IPS panels deliver contrast ratios around 1000:1, which means dark backgrounds in office applications, video content, and creative work appear washed out and grey rather than truly dark. IPS Black's 2000:1 contrast ratio closes a meaningful portion of that gap without the color accuracy compromises or viewing angle limitations associated with VA panels.

In practice, the difference is visible and appreciated. Dark user interface themes, black letterbox bars in video content, and shadow detail in photography all benefit from the deeper black floor. Side-by-side with a conventional IPS monitor, the U2723D's panel looks noticeably richer and more dimensional. It is not OLED-level black depth—no edge-lit LCD is—but it represents the best IPS technology currently offers for contrast.

Peak brightness at 400 cd/m² is adequate for most office and studio environments, though it can struggle slightly in very bright rooms with direct sunlight. The DisplayHDR 400 certification is largely nominal; 400 nits peak brightness without local dimming delivers limited HDR benefit beyond SDR, and users should not purchase this monitor expecting a cinematic HDR experience.

## Color Accuracy

Color accuracy is where the U2723D distinguishes itself from the competition most definitively, earning a 9.5 in this category. Dell factory-calibrates each unit and ships it with an individual calibration report showing Delta E values across the color space. Out of the box, the U2723D achieves an average Delta E of under 2—a threshold that professional photographers, graphic designers, and video editors consider publication-ready without additional calibration.

The panel covers 100% of the sRGB color space and 98% of DCI-P3, meaning the monitor can faithfully reproduce virtually every color used in web content, print workflows, and modern digital media delivery. For professionals whose work is color-critical—retouching photography, color grading video, preparing print-ready artwork—this level of accuracy directly translates to fewer surprises between what you see on screen and what appears in final output.

The U2723D supports multiple preset color modes including sRGB emulation, Display P3, and standard modes. The sRGB mode properly clamps the gamut to prevent oversaturation when working with sRGB content, which is a detail some less expensive monitors handle poorly. Color uniformity across the panel is also strong, with minimal corner-to-corner brightness or tint variation.

## Connectivity

The connectivity package on the U2723D is one of its most compelling selling points for professional users, and it's what separates this monitor from less expensive alternatives with similar panel specifications. The full complement includes one Thunderbolt 4 upstream port, one Thunderbolt 4 downstream port (for daisy-chaining a second monitor or connecting high-bandwidth peripherals), one USB-C with 90W Power Delivery, one DisplayPort 1.4, one HDMI 2.0, and a USB hub with three USB-A 3.2 Gen 2 ports and one USB-C downstream port. A built-in RJ-45 Gigabit Ethernet port completes the picture.

The practical implication of this connectivity is transformative for laptop users. Connect a single Thunderbolt 4 cable between your MacBook Pro or ThinkPad and the U2723D, and you get 4K video output, 90W laptop charging, Ethernet, and access to all the USB hub ports—all through one cable. No separate dock required, no power adapter taking up an outlet, no cables sprawling across the desk. For users who move between desk and mobile environments regularly, this is a genuine quality-of-life upgrade.

The Thunderbolt 4 downstream port also enables daisy-chaining a second display at up to 4K resolution for users who need a multi-monitor setup without separate graphics card outputs—useful for MacBook users whose laptops support daisy-chaining via Thunderbolt.

## Productivity Features

Beyond display quality and connectivity, the U2723D includes several software and hardware features that increase productivity. Dell Display Manager software (Mac and Windows compatible) provides monitor preset management, PiP/PbP split-screen layouts for simultaneously viewing two inputs side by side, and easy switching between color presets. The on-screen display menu is clear and well-organized, navigated via dedicated physical buttons on the underside of the bezel.

The built-in USB hub functions as a full-featured peripheral hub—connecting a USB keyboard, USB mouse, USB storage device, and USB webcam simultaneously is no problem. The USB-C downstream port in the hub can charge accessories at up to 15W.

Dell backs the U2723D with a three-year Advanced Exchange Service warranty, which includes free on-site repair or replacement if a pixel defect or hardware failure occurs. This warranty coverage is meaningfully better than the one-year warranties common among gaming monitor competitors.

## Who Should Buy It?

The Dell U2723D is the right monitor for creative professionals, software developers, and knowledge workers who spend the majority of their time in productivity applications and demand color accuracy. Photographers editing RAW files, video editors color grading projects, graphic designers preparing deliverables for print and digital—all of these users will find the U2723D's factory calibration and IPS Black panel directly beneficial to their work quality.

It is also the ideal single-cable laptop monitor for anyone using a Thunderbolt 4-equipped laptop. The ability to replace a separate dock, power adapter, and monitor cable with one Thunderbolt connection represents both a convenience and cost saving.

The U2723D is not the right choice for gamers. Its 60Hz ceiling is a hard limitation—there is no overclocking path, no variable refresh rate gaming mode, and response times are tuned for color accuracy rather than speed. Anyone prioritizing gaming alongside productivity work should look at the LG 27GP950-B reviewed alongside this monitor, which offers a comparable color gamut alongside high refresh rate gaming capability.

## Verdict

The Dell UltraSharp U2723D is a near-perfect professional productivity monitor. IPS Black technology delivers contrast performance that standard IPS panels cannot match, factory calibration ensures accuracy out of the box, and the Thunderbolt 4 connectivity suite with 90W power delivery makes it the most capable single-cable workstation monitor at its price point. Its limitations—60Hz refresh rate, no speakers, modest HDR performance—are real but predictable trade-offs for a display built around professional accuracy rather than entertainment performance. For creative professionals and power laptop users, it is the benchmark at 27 inches.
